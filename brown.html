Brown Rice

Brown rice is a whole grain, meaning it contains the bran, germ, and endosperm of the rice kernel. This makes it more nutritious than white rice, which has had the bran and germ removed during processing. Brown rice is higher in fiber, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants than white rice.

Blood Sugar Benefits

Brown rice has a lower glycemic index than white rice. This means it causes a slower rise in blood sugar levels, which is beneficial for people with diabetes or prediabetes.

Heart Healthy Choice

The fiber and magnesium content in brown rice contribute to heart health by lowering bad cholesterol and blood pressure.

Cooking Considerations

Brown rice takes longer to cook than white rice, typically around 45 minutes. However, there are quick-cooking brown rice options available that cook in about 20 minutes.
